A Career Built on Learning

I'm a self-taught software developer in the making — with 8+ years of professional experience in customer-facing and leadership roles, a background in outdoor education, and a proven track record of adapting and excelling in demanding environments. This is the story of how I got here, and where I'm headed.

Career Timeline

  • Early Career & Education — From age 16, I developed strong customer-facing skills in retail at Currys. A passion for the outdoors led me to study Outdoor Leisure at Bicton College, where I earned a National Diploma and discovered a natural aptitude for teaching and mentoring others.
  • Instructing & International Experience — I spent several years as an outdoor pursuits instructor across France, Spain, Wales, and Austria — delivering kayaking, climbing, mountain biking, snowboarding, and stand-up paddleboarding sessions to diverse groups. This sharpened my ability to communicate complex information clearly and adapt quickly to changing conditions.
  • Cycling Industry & Management — Settling in Exeter in 2013, I joined Saddles and Paddles in 2015, progressing to Shop Manager by 2017. Over several years I led a small team, oversaw workshop operations, managed stock and supplier relationships, and consistently delivered high standards of customer service. This role developed my systematic thinking, technical problem-solving, and operational leadership capabilities.
  • Transition into Software Development — Motivated by a desire for a more intellectually challenging and future-proof career, I began learning to program in November 2022 — working with a professional tutor and investing significant personal time outside of full-time work and family commitments. I have been building projects, developing this site, and steadily expanding my technical skill set ever since.
  • What Drives Me — My partner and I have two young children (born 2020 and 2023). The discipline and focus required to support a growing family while pursuing a career change has only strengthened my commitment. I am looking for a role where I can contribute meaningfully from day one, continue learning rapidly, and build a long-term career in technology.

Transferable Strengths

Leadership

Years managing a retail and workshop team — responsible for hiring, training, day-to-day scheduling, and performance under pressure.

Problem Solving

Bicycle diagnostics is methodical, systematic work. I've spent years breaking down complex mechanical faults into logical steps — a mindset that maps naturally to debugging and software design.

Communication

A decade of customer-facing and instructing experience has made clear, patient, and effective communication second nature — whether with a client, a team, or a learner.

Self-Motivation

Learning to code around a full-time job and a young family demands genuine commitment and discipline — skills I've demonstrated consistently throughout this transition.

Interests & Personal Projects

  • Mountain biking and road cycling — active participant and former industry professional
  • Surfing, climbing, and stand-up paddleboarding
  • Spending time outdoors with family and our dog along the Devon coast
